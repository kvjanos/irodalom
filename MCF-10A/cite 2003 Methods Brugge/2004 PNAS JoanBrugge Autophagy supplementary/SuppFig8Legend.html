Tumor necrosis factor-related apoptosis-inducing ligand (TRAIL) is required for induction of autophagy during lumen formation in vitro






Supporting Figure 8

Fig. 8. Tumor necrosis factor-related apoptosis-inducing ligand (TRAIL)-induced autophagy is inhibited by 3-MA, but not by zVAD•fluoromethyl ketone (fmk). (a) MCF-10A cells were plated in plastic coverslips and treated with 50 ng/ml recombinant human TRAIL alone, with TRAIL and zVAD•fmk, or with TRAIL and 3-MA for 24 h and then analyzed by using transmission electron microscopy (TEM). Arrows indicate autophagic vacuoles. Scale bars, 200 nm. zVAD•fmk treatment inhibited TRAIL-induced caspase 3 activation (52% vs. 16%; data not shown). (b) Quantification of the percentage of cells containing five or more autophagic vacuoles after each treatment. Autophagic vacuoles were defined as double-membrane structures enclosing cytoplasmic organelles. A minimum of 15 cells was counted for each of three independent experiments. P values were calculated by using Fisher’s exact test, comparing values to vehicle-treated samples. Error bars represent SD.
